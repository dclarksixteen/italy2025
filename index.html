Weather Forecast

Italian Weather in June

June brings warm and sunny weather to Italy. Temperatures typically range from 18°C to 28°C (65°F to 82°F) with occasional light showers. Evenings can be cooler, especially in northern regions.

What to Pack

  • Light, breathable clothing
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Light jacket for evenings
  • Umbrella or light raincoat
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses

Weather Tips

  • Stay hydrated in the summer heat
  • Many museums are air-conditioned
  • Expect brief afternoon showers
  • Italian businesses often close during midday heat
  • Plan indoor activities for the hottest hours

Food & Drink Guide

Dining Tips in Italy

Coperto: Many restaurants charge a small cover charge per person
Tipping: Not required but rounding up the bill is appreciated
Water: Ask for "acqua naturale" (still) or "acqua frizzante" (sparkling)
Coffee: Cappuccino is only consumed at breakfast, espresso after meals
Meal times: Lunch 1-3pm, Dinner 8-10pm (earlier is considered touristy)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pasta alla Carbonara

    Classic Roman pasta with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper.

  • Bistecca alla Fiorentina

    Thick T-bone steak grilled over chestnut wood, served rare.

  • Cicchetti

    Venetian small plates, like tapas, served in traditional bacari wine bars.

  • Gelato

    Italian ice cream with intense flavors and smooth texture.

Restaurants to Visit

  • Armando al Pantheon (Rome)

    Traditional Roman cuisine in a family-run restaurant near the Pantheon.

  • All'Antico Vinaio (Florence)

    Famous sandwich shop with enormous, flavorful panini.

  • Osteria Bancogiro (Venice)

    Overlooking the Grand Canal with excellent Venetian seafood.

Fun Facts & Useful Information

Language

While Italian is the official language, English is widely spoken in tourist areas. Learning a few basic phrases like "Grazie" (Thank you) and "Per favore" (Please) is appreciated.

Money

Italy uses the Euro (€). ATMs are widely available. While credit cards are accepted at most establishments, small shops and cafes may prefer cash.

Opening Hours

Many shops and businesses close for a few hours in the afternoon (riposo). Restaurants typically open for dinner around 7:30pm.

Etiquette

Greet people with "Buongiorno" (Good day) or "Buonasera" (Good evening). Dress modestly when visiting churches. Avoid loud behavior in public places.

Did You Know?

  • Italy has the most UNESCO World Heritage Sites of any country (58).
  • The first university in Europe, University of Bologna, was established in Italy in 1088.
  • Venice is built on 118 small islands connected by about 400 bridges.
  • The Vatican City is the smallest sovereign state in the world.
  • Italians consume over 60 liters of wine per person annually.
  • Rome has more than 2,000 fountains, more than any other city in the world.
