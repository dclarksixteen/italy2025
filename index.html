Weather Forecast

Southern Italian Weather in May

May is an excellent time to visit Southern Italy. The weather is pleasantly warm but not too hot, with temperatures typically ranging from 14°C to 24°C (57°F to 75°F). The Amalfi Coast and Capri enjoy mild Mediterranean conditions, while Rome can be slightly warmer.

What to Pack

  • Light, breathable clothing
  • Comfortable walking shoes for cobblestone streets
  • Light jacket or sweater for evenings
  • Swimwear (for Amalfi Coast and Capri)
  • Sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat
  • Small umbrella (occasional spring showers)

Weather Tips

  • May typically has 7-8 hours of sunshine daily
  • Sea temperatures are around 19°C (66°F) - swimmable
  • Occasional brief spring showers possible
  • Capri's Blue Grotto may close in rough seas
  • Bring a light layer for boat trips (can be breezy)
  • Rome can be warmer than coastal areas

Food & Drink Guide

Dining Tips in Southern Italy

Meal Times: Lunch 1-3pm, Dinner typically after 8pm
Coperto: Small cover charge is common in restaurants
Tipping: Not required but rounding up is appreciated (5-10%)
Water: Ask for "acqua naturale" (still) or "acqua frizzante" (sparkling)
Coffee: Espresso after meals is traditional; cappuccino only at breakfast

Naples & Amalfi Coast Specialties

  • Pizza Napoletana

    The original Neapolitan pizza with San Marzano tomatoes and buffalo mozzarella. Naples is the birthplace of pizza!

  • Scialatielli ai Frutti di Mare

    Fresh ribbon pasta with seafood, a specialty of the Amalfi Coast.

  • Delizia al Limone

    Lemon-infused sponge cake filled with lemon cream, featuring the famous Amalfi lemons.

  • Sfogliatella

    Shell-shaped filled pastry originating from Naples, with sweet ricotta filling.

Capri Specialties

  • Insalata Caprese

    The classic salad of tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil that originated on this island.

  • Ravioli Capresi

    Light pasta filled with caciotta cheese, marjoram, and served with a simple tomato sauce.

  • Torta Caprese

    Flourless chocolate and almond cake that is a specialty of the island.

  • Limoncello

    Lemon liqueur traditionally served as a digestif after meals.

Rome Specialties

  • Cacio e Pepe

    Pasta with Pecorino Romano cheese and black pepper - a creamy, simple Roman classic.

  • Carbonara

    Pasta with egg, Pecorino Romano, pancetta, and pepper (never with cream!).

  • Supplì

    Fried rice balls with a heart of mozzarella - a popular Roman street food.

  • Saltimbocca alla Romana

    Veal wrapped with prosciutto and sage, cooked in white wine and butter.

Recommended Restaurants

  • L'Antica Pizzeria da Michele (Naples)

    Historic pizzeria serving the authentic Neapolitan pizza since 1870.

  • La Tagliata (Positano, Amalfi Coast)

    Family-run restaurant with stunning views and farm-to-table cuisine.

  • Da Paolino (Capri)

    Famous for dining under lemon trees with traditional Caprese dishes.

  • Roscioli (Rome)

    Celebrated restaurant/bakery/deli offering the best carbonara and cacio e pepe in Rome.

Activity Planning Tips

Transportation Tips

  • Book high-speed trains in advance for savings
  • SITA buses connect towns along the Amalfi Coast
  • Ferries to Capri can be crowded - arrive early
  • Rome has excellent public transportation
  • Consider private transfers for Amalfi Coast convenience
  • Bring motion sickness remedies for winding coastal roads

General Tips

  • Book Vatican and Colosseum tours ahead
  • Blue Grotto visits are weather-dependent
  • Carry water and wear comfortable shoes
  • Modest dress required for churches (shoulders/knees covered)
  • May is less crowded than summer months
  • Many sites close on Mondays

Amalfi Coast Highlights

  • Scenic Drive Along SS163

    The famous winding coastal road with breathtaking views at every turn.

  • Positano's Colorful Village

    Explore the vertical town with its pastel-colored houses cascading down to the sea.

  • Ravello's Gardens

    Visit Villa Rufolo and Villa Cimbrone for spectacular views and gorgeous gardens.

  • Path of the Gods

    Scenic hiking trail between Bomerano and Nocelle with panoramic coastal views.

Capri Highlights

  • Blue Grotto (Grotta Azzurra)

    Famous sea cave where sunlight creates a magical blue reflection in the water.

  • Monte Solaro

    Take the chairlift to the highest point on the island for spectacular 360° views.

  • Gardens of Augustus

    Terraced gardens offering stunning views of the Faraglioni rock formations.

  • Capri Town & Marina Grande

    Explore the chic Piazzetta and shop along the upscale boutique-lined streets.

Naples Highlights

  • Historic Center (UNESCO site)

    Explore the ancient grid of streets with countless historical churches and monuments.

  • Naples National Archaeological Museum

    Houses one of the world's best collections of Greco-Roman artifacts.

  • Spaccanapoli

    The straight, narrow street that bisects the old historic center of Naples.

  • Pizza Making Experience

    Learn to make authentic Neapolitan pizza in its birthplace.

Rome Highlights

  • Vatican Museums & Sistine Chapel

    World-class art collection culminating in Michelangelo's masterpiece ceiling.

  • Colosseum & Roman Forum

    Ancient amphitheater and historical center of Roman public life.

  • Trevi Fountain

    Rome's largest Baroque fountain, famous for coin-tossing tradition.

  • Pantheon

    Ancient Roman temple with the world's largest unreinforced concrete dome.

Fun Facts & Useful Information

Language

Italian is the official language. English is widely spoken in tourist areas, especially in Rome. Learning a few basic phrases like "Grazie" (Thank you), "Per favore" (Please), and "Buongiorno" (Good day) is always appreciated.

Money

Italy uses the Euro (€). ATMs are widely available in all destinations. While credit cards are accepted at most establishments, smaller shops, cafes, and restaurants on the Amalfi Coast may prefer cash.

Transportation

The Amalfi Coast roads are narrow and winding. Ferries connect coastal towns and Capri. Rome has excellent public transportation with metro, buses, and trams. Always validate tickets before boarding trains and buses.

Etiquette

Greet people with "Buongiorno" (Good day) or "Buonasera" (Good evening). Dress modestly when visiting churches (shoulders and knees covered). Italians typically eat dinner later (8-9pm). Avoid loud behavior in public places.

Did You Know?

  • The Amalfi Coast road (SS163) is considered one of the most scenic drives in the world.
  • Capri was a favorite retreat of Roman emperors and has ruins of Imperial Roman villas.
  • Naples' historic center is the largest in Europe and a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • The famous Limoncello liqueur is made from Amalfi Coast lemons, which can grow as large as grapefruits.
  • Positano was a poor fishing village until the 1950s when it became a fashionable resort.
  • The Blue Grotto in Capri was used as a marine temple during the Roman period.
  • The Vatican City in Rome is the smallest sovereign state in the world.
  • Rome has more than 2,000 fountains, more than any other city in the world.
